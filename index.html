WHAT IS DEPRESSION?

Overview of Depression

Major depressive disorder, sometimes known as depression, is a serious medical condition that frequently affects people's feelings, thoughts, and behaviours. The main symptom of depression is long-term persistent depression, which will affect the daily life of patients. Mild depression can only be depressed, which may not affect daily life, but it may make patients feel difficult and meaningless. In the most serious case, depression can threaten life and make patients suicidal.

Symptoms of Depression

Depression may only occur once in a lifetime, but usually occurs multiple times. During the attack, symptoms appear almost most of the day, which may include:

  • Feel depressed, teary, lonely, or hopeless.
  • Unexplained physical issues such headaches or backaches.
  • Anger, irritability, or frustration can be sparked by even small things.
  • Loss of enjoyment or interest in most of the activities.
  • Sleep disorders, including insomnia or drowsiness
  • Exhausted. Even if you do a few tiny tasks, it's challenging.
  • Lack of appetite and weight loss, or overeating to put on weight
  • Feel guilty, feel worthless, and disappointed with yourself.
  • It is challenging to concentrate, make decisions, and remember things.
  • Considerations about suicide appears.

    • Types of Depression

    According to NHS, there are 3 level of depression stage, mild, moderate or serve, and 5 types of depression:

      Clinical depression
      Psychotic depression
      Postnatal depression
      seasonal affective disorder (SAD)

    We provide Counselling.

    Psychotherapy for Depression

    It's important to seek help from a doctor if you think you may be depressed. Schedule an appointment or visit a mental health professional as soon as you feel depressed.

    Online Therapy

    If you are nervous about seeing a specialist and prefer to discuss your concerns in a familiar place, a telephone or online consultation may be the right option for you.

    Medication for depression

    Although medication is typically not a long-term solution, it may help with some of the symptoms. The effect of drugs varies from person to person. You can discuss your treatment plan with your doctor first.

    Self-Help

    Self-help may be the first treatment choice provided by the doctor, especially if you experience mild depression. This is because it can be carried out immediately, and there is a chance to make you feel better directly.
