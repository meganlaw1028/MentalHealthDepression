Overview of Depression

Depression is a mood disorder that can make people feel sad and lose interest. Depression, also known as severe depression or clinical depression, can affect your feelings, thinking and behaviors, and may lead to various emotional and physical problems. You may have difficulty doing normal daily activities, and sometimes you may feel that life is worthless. Depression is not only a depression attack, but also a personality weakness, and it cannot be easily rid of it in the way of "chopping the mess with a quick knife". Depression may require long-term treatment. But don't be discouraged. Most people with depression will feel better after receiving drug treatment, psychotherapy or two-pronged therapy.

Symptoms of Depression

Depression may only occur once in a lifetime, but usually occurs multiple times. During the attack, symptoms appear almost most of the day, which may include:

  • Feel depressed, teary, lonely, or hopeless.
  • Unexplained physical issues such headaches or backaches.
  • Anger, irritability, or frustration can be sparked by even the smallest things.
  • Loss of enjoyment or interest in the majority or all of the typical activities, such as hobbies, sports, or sex.
  • Sleep disorders, including insomnia or drowsiness
  • Exhausted. Even if you do a few tiny tasks, it's challenging.
  • Lack of appetite and weight loss, or overeating to put on weight
  • Feel useless or guilty, and whine or blame past mistakes on yourself.
  • It is challenging to think clearly, concentrate,make decisions, and remember things.
  • Considerations about suicide, death, or attempted or actual suicide on a regular basis or repeatedly.
